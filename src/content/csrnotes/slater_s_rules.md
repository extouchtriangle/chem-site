Slater's rules are a way to estimate the effective nuclear charge of an electron.


## Slater's rules

1.  Split the orbitals into groups as follows: (1s) (2s, 2p) (3s, 3p) (3d) (4s, 4p) (4d) (4f) ...
2.  In the list, no contribution is given to orbitals to the right of the electron whose Z<sub>eff</sub> (or S) we want to find.
3.  If we are considering a s or p electron, electrons in the next lowest shell contribute 0.85 to S. Electrons in lower shells contribute 1.00 to S.
4.  For each electron in the same group as the one considered, add 0.35. (Add 0.30 in the specific case of the 1s group).
5.  If we are considering a d or f electron, all electrons below it in energy contribute 1.00 to S.
