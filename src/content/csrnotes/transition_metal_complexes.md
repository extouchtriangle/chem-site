Transition metals readily form complex ions.
In 'classical' complex ions, a metal cation is surrounded by either anions (halides, cyanide) or electronegative atoms with lone pairs like NH<sub>3</sub> and OH<sub>2</sub>.


## Geometries of complexes {#geometries-of-complexes}

-   Four-coordinate complexes are tetrahedral, or rarely square-planar is the case of Group 10 (like Pd)
-   Six-coordinate complexes are octahedral.
