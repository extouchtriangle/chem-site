-   As orbitals are filled from lowest energy to highest energy, the 4/s/ orbital is filled before the 3d orbital because it penetrates more effectively.
-   However, in transition metals the 3/d/ orbitals are  **less** energetic than the 4/s/. This is because the 4/s/ electrons don't shield the nuclear charge well for the 3/d/, meaning that the increase in Z<sub>eff</sub> quickly decreases the energy of the _d_ orbitals.[^fn:1]
-   As you definitely already know, Cr and Cu are exceptions: prioritizes half filled _d_ shells over 2 electrons in _s_ shell

[^fn:1]: Note: this is only for electrons occupied in **ground-state** configurations; if the _s_ electrons moved to the _d_ subshell the orbital energy would end up greater than the ground-state configuration because of electron-electron repulsion.
