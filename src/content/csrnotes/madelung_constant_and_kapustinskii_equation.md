The Kapustinskii equation is way to roughly estimate the lattice energy of an ionic compound.


## Madelung constant {#madelung-constant}

The Madelung constant represents the attractive force that a single ion in an ionic lattice feels --- it feels attraction to the neighboring anions, but then repulsion from the next-closest cations, and so on.


## The Kapustinskii equation {#the-kapustinskii-equation}

M / n<sub>ions</sub> &asymp; 0.87
So, E<sub>lattice</sub> can be calculated via
E<sub>lattice</sub> = -N<sub>A</sub>M &times; z<sub>+</sub>z<sub>-</sub>e<sup>2</sup>/4&pi;&epsilon;<sub>0</sub>r<sub>e</sub> (1 - 1/n)

| Variable                     | Definition                                            |
|------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------|
| E<sub>lattice</sub>          | Lattice energy                                        |
| M                            | Madelung constant                                     |
| N<sub>A</sub>                | Avogadro constant                                     |
| z<sub>+</sub>, z<sub>-</sub> | Charges of cation and anion (in elementary charges)   |
| e                            | Elementary charge; i. e. charge of proton or electron |
| &epsilon;<sub>0</sub>        | Permittivity of free space                            |
| r<sub>e</sub>                | Equilibrium distance between cation and anion         |
| n                            | Born exponent correction term, typically around 9     |
