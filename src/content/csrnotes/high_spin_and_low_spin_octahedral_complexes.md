Depending on the value of [&Delta;<sub>0</sub>]({{< relref "factors_affecting_ligand_field_splitting.md" >}}), a complex can be either ****high-spin**** or ****low-spin****, as shown.
![](/ox-hugo/high-low-spin.png)
Formally, high-spin arrangements have more electrons with parallel spins, and low-spin arrangements have less electrons with parallel spins.


## Deciding between high-spin and low-spin

In low-spin complexes, &Delta;<sub>0</sub> outweighs the exchange interaction. For example, for the low-spin to be favored over high spin (in the above image):

<style>.org-center { margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center; }</style>

<div class="org-center">

energy of low spin &lt; energy of high spin
-4K &lt; 2&Delta;<sub>0</sub> - 10K
&Delta;<sub>0</sub> &gt; 3K

</div>

For example, [Fe(H<sub>2</sub>O)<sub>6</sub>]<sup>3+</sup> is high-spin, while [Fe(CN)<sub>6</sub>]<sup>3-</sup> is low-spin.
