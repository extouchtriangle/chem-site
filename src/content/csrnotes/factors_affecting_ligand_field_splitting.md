A number of factors affect the value of [&Delta;<sub>0</sub>]({{< relref "octahedral_complexes.md" >}}), also known as the ligand-field splitting, in [complex ions]({{< relref "coordination_chemistry.md" >}}).


## The ligand

&Delta;<sub>0</sub> appears to increase from left to right in the following list of ligands:

<style>.org-center { margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center; }</style>

<div class="org-center">

I<sup>-</sup>, Br<sup>-</sup>, S<sup>2-</sup>, SCN<sup>-</sup>, Cl<sup>-</sup>, F<sup>-</sup>, OH<sup>-</sup>, H<sub>2</sub>O, NH<sub>3</sub>, PPh<sub>3</sub>, CN<sup>-</sup>, CO

</div>

Increasing the strength of the &sigma; interactions increases &Delta;<sub>0</sub>, but increasing the strength of the &pi; interaction decreases &Delta;_0.
Usually, factors that increase the &sigma; interaction increase the &pi; interaction too, but the &sigma; interaction is so much stronger that the &sigma; interaction usually determines the &Delta;<sub>0</sub>.


## The metal cation

Usually, &Delta;<sub>0</sub> increases as the oxidation state of the metal increases and from left to right across a period.
This happens because the 3/d/ AOs on the cation decrease in energy, matching the energy of the ligand orbitals better. Also, this contraction of the AOs promotes better overlap.
