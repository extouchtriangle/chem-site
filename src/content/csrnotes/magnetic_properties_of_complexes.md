[Complex ions]({{< relref "coordination_chemistry.md" >}}) often have interesting magnetic properties.


## Paramagnetism

For the first-row transition metals, the paramagnetism is proportional to [n(n+2)]<sup>1/2</sup>, where _n_ is the number of unpaired electrons.
This is one way to distinguish between [high-spin and low-spin complexes]({{< relref "high_spin_and_low_spin_octahedral_complexes.md" >}}); high-spin complexes usually have much stronger paramagnetism.
For example, the difference between high-spin /d/<sup>5</sup> complexes and low-spin /d/<sup>5</sup> complexes is a factor of [(5 &times; 7) / (1 &times; 3)]<sup>1/2</sup> = 3.4, which is easily detectable.

When n=0, the complex is diamagnetic and are repelled from a magnetic field gradient. However, this effect is much weaker than paramagnetism.
